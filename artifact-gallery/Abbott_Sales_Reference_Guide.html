Medical Devices — $20B+ Segment

Abbott's largest and fastest-growing segment. Twelve consecutive quarters of double-digit organic growth through Q4 2025. Key sub-segments: Diabetes Care, Electrophysiology, Structural Heart, Heart Failure, Rhythm Management, Vascular, Neuromodulation.

Diabetes Care — CGM

FreeStyle Libre Platform

Sensor-based continuous glucose monitoring (CGM) system that replaces painful finger-sticks. Measures interstitial glucose via a small subcutaneous filament using glucose oxidase enzyme electrochemistry. The sensor generates an electrical signal proportional to glucose concentration, transmitted wirelessly to a reader or smartphone app.

Key variants: Libre 3 (14-day wear, thinnest sensor), Libre 2 Plus, Lingo (OTC biowearable for non-diabetics), Libre Rio (OTC CGM). Upcoming: dual-analyte sensor adding ketone monitoring to prevent DKA.

FY2025 CGM: ~$7.5B+ ~7M users globally ~60% US CGM share Org growth: ~20%

Consumer Experience: Apply sensor to back of upper arm (painless 1-second applicator). Scan with phone or reader for real-time glucose data. 14-day wear per sensor. Libre 3 streams data continuously — no manual scans needed. Alarms for highs and lows. App provides AGP reports to share with providers.

Provider Adoption: Endocrinologists are primary prescribers (~85% CGM adoption among endo patients). PCPs are fastest-growing prescriber category but still underpenetrated (~30% adoption). Key selling points for physicians: reduced HbA1c, time-in-range data, fewer hypoglycemic events, AGP reports that save consultation time. Integration with AID insulin pump systems is expanding (several partnerships announced for 2025).

⚠ RECALL NOTE (Nov 2025): Class I FDA recall on certain Libre 3/3 Plus sensor lots (~3M sensors) due to incorrectly low glucose readings. 860 serious injuries, 7 deaths reported as of Jan 7, 2026. Abbott says root cause identified and resolved (single production line issue). No supply disruption expected. Sales reps must be prepared to address this proactively.

Electrophysiology — Ablation

Volt™ PFA System

FDA-approved December 2025 for treating atrial fibrillation (AFib). Uses pulsed field ablation (PFA) — targeted, high-energy electrical pulses that selectively destroy cardiac tissue causing arrhythmia while sparing nearby structures (esophagus, phrenic nerve). Unique balloon-in-basket catheter design allows mapping, pacing, and ablation with a single device.

Chemistry/Physics: PFA delivers nanosecond-to-microsecond electrical pulses that create irreversible electroporation — permanent pores in cardiac cell membranes causing cell death. Unlike radiofrequency ablation (RFA) (thermal), PFA is tissue-selective for cardiomyocytes.

Works with: EnSite™ X EP System for 3D cardiac mapping.

US EP market: ~$10B EP growth: ~10–13% Limited market release 2026

Provider Adoption: Electrophysiologists are the sole prescribers. Selling points: single-catheter workflow (vs. multi-catheter competitors), tissue selectivity reduces collateral damage risk, real-time contact visualization. Approved for both paroxysmal and persistent AFib. CE Mark obtained earlier in 2025 for European sites.

Structural Heart — TEER

MitraClip / TriClip / Navitor

MitraClip: Transcatheter edge-to-edge repair (TEER) device for mitral regurgitation. A cobalt-chromium clip delivered via catheter through the femoral vein clips the mitral valve leaflets to reduce backflow, avoiding open-heart surgery. G5 is the latest generation.

TriClip: Same TEER concept applied to the tricuspid valve. First-of-its-kind FDA-approved device. 15,000+ patients treated as of 2025.

Navitor: Transcatheter aortic valve replacement (TAVR) system — a prosthetic valve made of bovine pericardial tissue mounted on a nitinol frame, delivered via catheter to replace a diseased aortic valve.

Structural Heart: ~$2.4B (2025) Org growth: ~13% Double-digit Navitor growth

Provider Adoption: Interventional cardiologists and cardiac surgeons. Selling points: avoidance of open-heart surgery, shorter recovery, growing real-world evidence for secondary MR. Procedure volumes growing double-digits in the US driven by expanded indications and surgeon training programs.

Heart Failure / Rhythm

AVEIR™ Leadless Pacemaker & Amplatzer™ Amulet™

AVEIR: Leadless pacemaker implanted directly into the heart via catheter — no surgical pocket, no transvenous leads. Titanium capsule with steroid-eluting dexamethasone-coated electrode to reduce inflammation at the tissue interface.

Amplatzer Amulet: Left atrial appendage closure (LAAC) device for stroke prevention in AFib patients who cannot tolerate long-term anticoagulants. Nitinol mesh with polyester fabric to seal off the appendage.

HF sales: ~$1.4B (2025) Rhythm Mgmt: ~$2.5B $10B rhythm market opp.
Neuromodulation

Neuromodulation — Chronic Pain

Spinal cord stimulation (SCS) and dorsal root ganglion (DRG) stimulation devices for chronic pain. Implanted pulse generators deliver electrical impulses to the spinal cord to interrupt pain signals. Uses proprietary BurstDR™ stimulation waveform.

Neuromod: ~$960M (2025) Growth: ~3%

Established Pharmaceuticals (EPD) — ~$5.2B

Branded generic medicines sold exclusively in international and emerging markets (no US pharma sales). Key geographies: India, China, Brazil, Russia, Latin America, Southeast Asia, Middle East, Africa. Abbott separated its innovative pharma business as AbbVie in 2013; this segment focuses on high-quality branded generics and, increasingly, biosimilars.

Anti-inflammatory / Pain

Brufen (Ibuprofen)

NSAID that inhibits cyclooxygenase (COX-1/COX-2) enzymes, reducing prostaglandin synthesis. Used for pain, fever, and inflammation. Chemical formula: C₁₃H₁₈O₂. A propionic acid derivative.

Target brand: Advil/Motrin (J&J). Ibuprofen is long off-patent. Abbott competes on brand trust, formulation quality, and distribution reach in emerging markets. TAM: Global OTC analgesics market ~$30B+.

Endocrine / Thyroid

Synthroid (Levothyroxine Sodium)

Synthetic form of thyroid hormone T4 (thyroxine). Chemical formula: C₁₅H₁₀I₄NNaO₄. Replaces deficient thyroid hormone in hypothyroidism. Narrow therapeutic index — small dose changes cause significant clinical effects.

Target brand: Synthroid itself is the reference brand (originally from Knoll/BASF, now Abbott). Generic levothyroxine competitors: Mylan, Sandoz. LOE: Long off-patent. Competition is on formulation consistency. TAM: Global hypothyroidism treatment ~$3B.

Gastroenterology

Creon (Pancreatin)

Pancreatic enzyme replacement therapy (PERT). Contains lipase, protease, and amylase derived from porcine pancreas. Helps digest fats, proteins, and carbohydrates in patients with exocrine pancreatic insufficiency (EPI) — common in cystic fibrosis and chronic pancreatitis.

TAM: Global PERT market ~$2.5B. Chronic refill — patients need PERT with every meal for life. Rx renewal: Typically 90-day supply, auto-renewed indefinitely.

GI / Laxative

Duphalac (Lactulose)

Synthetic disaccharide (C₁₂H₂₂O₁₁) not absorbed in the upper GI tract. Reaches the colon where bacteria ferment it into short-chain fatty acids, drawing water into the bowel via osmotic effect. Used for constipation and hepatic encephalopathy (reduces ammonia absorption).

Cardiology / Lipids

Lipanthyl (Fenofibrate)

Fibrate that activates PPARα receptors, increasing lipoprotein lipase activity. Reduces triglycerides and raises HDL cholesterol. Chemical formula: C₂₀H₂₁ClO₄. Used alongside statins for mixed dyslipidemia.

Target brand: Tricor/Trilipix (AbbVie). Off-patent. TAM: Global lipid-lowering drugs ~$25B (dominated by statins, but fibrates maintain niche for hypertriglyceridemia).

Biosimilars — New Growth

Biosimilars (mAbxience Partnership)

Abbott partnered with mAbxience (2018, expanded 2023) to commercialize monoclonal antibody biosimilars in oncology and women's health across Latin America, Southeast Asia, Middle East, and Africa. First molecules launched as early as 2025. Biosimilars are manufactured using CHO cells and purified to demonstrate comparable quality, safety, and efficacy to reference biologics.

Why it matters: Biosimilars are the fastest-growing segment in branded generics. They bring affordable access to complex biologic therapies (e.g., monoclonal antibodies for cancer) in markets where branded biologics may be unaffordable. mAbxience manufactures at GMP facilities in Spain and Argentina.

Nutritional Products — ~$8.6B

Pediatric nutrition (Similac, PediaSure) and adult nutrition (Ensure, Glucerna). Growth driven by aging demographics, chronic disease management, and emerging market expansion.

Pediatric Nutrition

Similac Infant Formula

Complete infant nutrition formula based on cow's milk protein (casein and whey), supplemented with DHA (docosahexaenoic acid, an omega-3 fatty acid), ARA (arachidonic acid), human milk oligosaccharides (HMOs), lutein, vitamin E, and prebiotics. Lactose is the primary carbohydrate source.

Product lines: Similac Advance (standard), Similac Pro-Advance (with HMOs), Similac Sensitive (lactose-reduced), Similac Alimentum (extensively hydrolyzed for cow's milk allergy), Similac Special Care (NICU premature infants).

US Pediatric: ~$2.2B (2025) Int'l Pediatric: ~$1.8B

⚠ NEC LAWSUIT — ONGOING: As of April 2026, ~782 lawsuits are pending in MDL 3026 (Northern District of Illinois) alleging that cow's milk-based formulas (Similac, Enfamil) increase necrotizing enterocolitis (NEC) risk in premature/low-birth-weight infants. NEC is a life-threatening intestinal condition. Key developments:

  • State court verdicts: $60M (Illinois, March 2024); $495M (Missouri, July 2024); $53M (Chicago, April 2026); ~$32M (Connecticut, December 2025)
  • Federal MDL: Abbott prevailed via summary judgment in first 3 bellwether cases (Judge Pallmeyer ruled plaintiff expert testimony insufficient). More bellwethers scheduled Aug 2026, Nov 2026, Feb 2027
  • No global settlement reached. Settlement talks ongoing
  • Sales reps should NOT discuss litigation specifics with HCPs — refer inquiries to Abbott Legal
Adult Nutrition

Ensure / Glucerna

Ensure: Complete, balanced adult nutrition supplement. Provides protein (from milk protein concentrate and soy protein isolate), carbohydrates, fats, vitamins, and minerals. Used for general nutrition support, post-surgical recovery, and elderly nutrition.

Glucerna: Specifically designed for people with diabetes. Uses slow-release carbohydrate blend (modified maltodextrin, fructose) to minimize glycemic impact. Fortified with chromium (insulin sensitivity) and biotin.

US Adult: ~$1.4B (2025) Org growth: ~8–9% Market leader

Administration: OTC — sold directly to consumers via retail, pharmacy, and online. No prescription required. Also used in clinical/institutional settings (hospitals, nursing homes) for tube feeding and oral supplementation.

Pediatric Specialty

PediaSure / Pedialyte

PediaSure: Nutritional supplement for children 1–13 with growth or picky eating concerns. Contains 25 vitamins/minerals, protein, DHA, and prebiotics.

Pedialyte: Oral rehydration solution containing optimal electrolyte balance (sodium, potassium, chloride) and glucose for rehydration during illness. Based on WHO oral rehydration salt (ORS) principles. Also popular as a hangover remedy among adults.

Diagnostics — ~$9B Legacy + Exact Sciences

Core Laboratory, Molecular, Point of Care, Rapid Diagnostics. With the March 2026 closing of the Exact Sciences acquisition ($21B), Abbott now adds a cancer screening and precision oncology diagnostics vertical valued at $60B TAM.

Core Lab / Rapid

Alinity Systems & BinaxNOW

Alinity: Family of automated analyzers for clinical chemistry, immunoassay, hematology, and blood screening. High-throughput hospital/lab systems that run immunoassays using chemiluminescence.

BinaxNOW: Rapid antigen tests (lateral flow immunochromatographic assay) for COVID-19, influenza, RSV, strep. Point-of-care format — results in 15 minutes. COVID testing revenue normalizing post-pandemic.

Cancer Diagnostics — NEW (Exact Sciences)

Cologuard / Oncotype DX / CancerGuard

Cologuard: Non-invasive, at-home stool-based test for colorectal cancer (CRC) screening. Detects altered DNA biomarkers (methylated BMP3, NDRG4 genes) and hemoglobin in stool samples via PCR amplification. FDA-approved for average-risk adults 45+. Every 3 years.

Oncotype DX: Genomic test analyzing expression of 21 genes in breast cancer tissue to predict recurrence risk and chemotherapy benefit. Uses RT-PCR on FFPE tumor samples.

CancerGuard: Multi-cancer early detection (MCED) blood test. OncoDetect: Molecular residual disease (MRD) monitoring.

TAM: $60B US ~$3B incremental rev. 2026 Closed March 23, 2026

Headwinds & Tailwinds

What keeps us up at night — and what gets us excited.

🔴 Scary Headwinds

FreeStyle Libre 3 Class I Recall FDA linked 860 serious injuries and 7 deaths to faulty Libre 3/3 Plus sensors (falsely low glucose readings). ~3M sensors recalled. Brand equity risk in an $8B franchise. FDA also issued a warning letter citing quality system violations at the Alameda manufacturing facility.
NEC Litigation Exposure ~782 lawsuits pending in MDL 3026. State court verdicts totaling $500M+ already. If a global settlement is reached, liability could be in the billions. While Abbott has prevailed in federal bellwethers so far, the April 2026 Chicago verdict ($53M) and ongoing trials create headline risk.
CGM Competitive Pressure Dexcom G7 and Stelo (OTC), plus Medtronic's Simplera, are closing the gap. Pricing pressure as CGM becomes mass-market. Libre's US share (~60%) may face erosion.
Diagnostics Normalization Post-COVID COVID testing revenue continues to fade. Core diagnostics growing but not fast enough to fully offset. This segment underperformed in recent quarters.
Nutrition Segment Softness Q4 2025 nutrition sales missed expectations by ~7% YoY. Pricing pressure, rising input costs, and volume softness. Recovery expected in H2 2026 but uncertain timing.
$20B Debt Load from Exact Sciences Abbott raised $20B in senior notes to finance the acquisition. While manageable given Abbott's cash flow, it increases leverage and limits near-term financial flexibility. Integration execution risk.
FX / Macro / Tariff Headwinds ~55% of revenue is international. Strong dollar, emerging market currency volatility, inflation, and potential tariff impacts all weigh on margins. China regulatory delays (Libre 3 approval pending).
Reimbursement / Medicare Risk Any future CMS cuts to device reimbursement (especially DME for CGM or DRG for structural heart) would directly impact revenue. Healthcare reform uncertainty.

🟢 Exciting Tailwinds

CGM Expansion — Massive Underpenetration 500M+ people with diabetes globally; only ~10M use CGMs. 25% of intensive insulin users in the US still not on CGM; 50% internationally. Basal-only and Type 2 non-insulin coverage expansion is a huge unlock. Target: $10B in FreeStyle Libre sales by 2028.
Volt PFA US Launch — New EP Growth Engine PFA is the next generation of AFib ablation. $10B addressable EP market. Abbott's unique single-catheter design could gain significant share. UBS expects EP reacceleration in 2026.
Exact Sciences — Cancer Diagnostics Vertical $21B acquisition closed March 2026. Adds ~$3B incremental revenue. Entry into $60B US cancer screening/precision oncology market. Cologuard has strong PCP adoption and ACA-mandated coverage.
Dual-Analyte CGM Sensor (Glucose + Ketone) Upcoming launch integrates ketone monitoring into CGM. First combined glucose/ketone sensor on the market. Key differentiator for intensive insulin users at risk of DKA.
Structural Heart Momentum MitraClip, TriClip (first-of-its-kind), and Navitor all posting double-digit growth. Expanding indications. Aging population driving procedure volumes. G5 system enhances physician workflow.
Biosimilars in Emerging Markets Fastest-growing segment of branded generics. mAbxience partnership launching oncology and women's health biosimilars. Abbott's distribution in 160+ countries provides unmatched reach.
OTC Biowearables (Lingo) Leveraging Libre technology for wellness/non-diabetes consumers. Entirely new market segment. National expansion underway. No prescription required.
Dividend King Status + Cash Flow 54-year consecutive dividend increase streak. 2025 FCF supports ongoing share buybacks, dividend growth (7% increase in 2025), and acquisitions. 2026 guidance: EPS $5.55–$5.80 (~10% growth), organic revenue growth 6.5–7.5%.

Glossary

Searchable reference for all technical terms, chemicals, products, and concepts used in this guide.

Product

FreeStyle Libre

Abbott's sensor-based continuous glucose monitoring (CGM) platform. Variants include Libre 2, Libre 2 Plus, Libre 3, Libre 3 Plus. Worn on the back of the upper arm for up to 14 days. Used by ~7 million people worldwide.

Product

Lingo

Abbott's OTC (over-the-counter) biowearable based on Libre technology. Targets non-diabetic consumers interested in tracking glucose for wellness, fitness, and metabolic health.

Product

Libre Rio

Abbott's OTC continuous glucose monitoring system for diabetes management without a prescription. FDA-cleared.

Product

Volt™ PFA System

Abbott's pulsed field ablation catheter system for treating atrial fibrillation. FDA-approved December 2025. Unique balloon-in-basket design allows mapping, pacing, and ablation with a single catheter.

Product

MitraClip

Abbott's transcatheter edge-to-edge repair (TEER) device for mitral regurgitation. Cobalt-chromium clip delivered via catheter to clip mitral valve leaflets, reducing backflow. G5 is the latest generation.

Product

TriClip

Abbott's TEER device for tricuspid regurgitation. First-of-its-kind FDA-approved transcatheter therapy for the tricuspid valve. Over 15,000 patients treated as of 2025.

Product

Navitor

Abbott's transcatheter aortic valve replacement (TAVR) system. A bovine pericardial tissue valve mounted on a nitinol frame, delivered via catheter to replace a diseased aortic valve.

Product

AVEIR™

Abbott's leadless pacemaker. Titanium capsule implanted directly into the heart via catheter — no surgical pocket or transvenous leads required. Steroid-eluting electrode to reduce inflammation.

Product

Amplatzer™ Amulet™

Left atrial appendage closure (LAAC) device for stroke prevention in AFib patients who cannot tolerate anticoagulants. Nitinol mesh with polyester fabric.

Product

EnSite™ X EP System

Abbott's 3D cardiac mapping system used during electrophysiology procedures. Creates real-time electrical maps of the heart to guide ablation catheter placement.

Product

Similac

Abbott's infant formula brand. Product lines include Advance, Pro-Advance (with HMOs), Sensitive (lactose-reduced), Alimentum (hydrolyzed), and Special Care (NICU). Subject to ongoing NEC litigation.

Product

Ensure

Abbott's market-leading adult nutritional supplement. Complete, balanced nutrition providing protein, carbohydrates, fats, vitamins, and minerals. Available in multiple formulations (Original, Plus, Max Protein, etc.).

Product

Glucerna

Abbott's nutritional supplement designed for people with diabetes. Uses slow-release carbohydrates to minimize glycemic impact. Fortified with chromium and biotin.

Product

PediaSure

Abbott's nutritional supplement for children (1–13 years) with growth concerns or picky eating. Contains 25 vitamins/minerals, protein, DHA, and prebiotics.

Product

Pedialyte

Abbott's oral rehydration solution for children and adults. Optimal electrolyte balance (sodium, potassium, chloride) with glucose for rehydration during illness.

Product

Brufen

Abbott's branded ibuprofen sold in international markets. NSAID for pain, fever, and inflammation. Competes with Advil/Motrin in emerging markets.

Product

Synthroid

Abbott's branded levothyroxine sodium. Synthetic T4 thyroid hormone replacement for hypothyroidism. Narrow therapeutic index — formulation consistency is critical.

Product

Creon

Abbott's pancreatic enzyme replacement therapy (PERT). Contains lipase, protease, and amylase derived from porcine pancreas. Used for exocrine pancreatic insufficiency.

Product

Duphalac

Abbott's branded lactulose. Osmotic laxative used for constipation and hepatic encephalopathy.

Product

Lipanthyl

Abbott's branded fenofibrate for lipid management. Reduces triglycerides and raises HDL cholesterol.

Product

Cologuard

Non-invasive stool-based colorectal cancer screening test (acquired via Exact Sciences, closed March 2026). Detects altered DNA biomarkers and hemoglobin. FDA-approved for average-risk adults 45+. Every 3 years.

Chemical

Ibuprofen (C₁₃H₁₈O₂)

A propionic acid derivative NSAID. Inhibits COX-1 and COX-2 enzymes, reducing prostaglandin synthesis and thereby decreasing pain, fever, and inflammation.

Chemical

Levothyroxine Sodium (C₁₅H₁₀I₄NNaO₄)

Synthetic thyroid hormone T4. Contains four iodine atoms on a tyrosine backbone. Converted in the body to the active T3 (triiodothyronine) form. Narrow therapeutic index.

Chemical

Pancreatin

A mixture of digestive enzymes (lipase, protease, amylase) derived from porcine pancreas. Used as enzyme replacement in exocrine pancreatic insufficiency.

Chemical

Lactulose (C₁₂H₂₂O₁₁)

Synthetic disaccharide (galactose + fructose). Not absorbed in upper GI tract. Fermented by colonic bacteria into short-chain fatty acids, creating osmotic laxative effect. Also reduces ammonia in hepatic encephalopathy.

Chemical

Fenofibrate (C₂₀H₂₁ClO₄)

A fibric acid derivative that activates PPARα nuclear receptors. Increases lipoprotein lipase activity, reducing triglycerides and raising HDL cholesterol.

Material

Nitinol

Nickel-titanium alloy with shape memory and superelastic properties. Widely used in medical devices (stents, heart valve frames, pacemaker components) because it can be compressed for catheter delivery and expand to its original shape in the body.

Chemical

Dexamethasone

A synthetic corticosteroid used as a coating on pacemaker electrodes (like AVEIR) to reduce inflammation at the tissue interface, lowering pacing thresholds.

Enzyme

Glucose Oxidase

Enzyme used in CGM sensor electrochemistry. Catalyzes oxidation of glucose to gluconic acid and hydrogen peroxide. The H₂O₂ generated is measured electrochemically to determine glucose concentration.

Chemical

DHA (Docosahexaenoic Acid)

An omega-3 long-chain polyunsaturated fatty acid critical for brain and eye development. Found in breast milk; added to infant formulas. Chemical formula: C₂₂H₃₂O₂.

Chemical

ARA (Arachidonic Acid)

An omega-6 fatty acid important for infant brain development and immune function. Added to infant formulas alongside DHA.

Mineral

Chromium

Trace mineral that enhances insulin sensitivity by potentiating insulin receptor signaling. Added to Glucerna products for diabetes management.

Vitamin

Biotin (Vitamin B7)

Water-soluble B vitamin involved in carbohydrate, fat, and protein metabolism. Added to nutritional supplements. Note: high biotin levels can interfere with certain lab assays (troponin, thyroid).

Biology / Device

Continuous Glucose Monitoring (CGM)

Technology that measures glucose levels continuously (typically every 1–5 minutes) via a small subcutaneous sensor. Replaces or supplements traditional finger-stick blood glucose testing.

Biology

Interstitial Glucose

Glucose concentration in the interstitial fluid (the fluid between cells). CGM sensors measure this rather than blood glucose directly. There is a ~5–15 minute physiological lag between blood and interstitial glucose.

Biology

HbA1c (Glycated Hemoglobin)

Blood test measuring average blood sugar over the past 2–3 months. Glucose binds to hemoglobin in red blood cells. Normal: <5.7%; Pre-diabetes: 5.7–6.4%; Diabetes: ≥6.5%. Key outcome metric for diabetes management.

Disease

Diabetes

Chronic metabolic disease characterized by elevated blood glucose. Type 1: autoimmune destruction of insulin-producing beta cells. Type 2: insulin resistance and progressive beta cell dysfunction. ~500 million people affected globally.

Condition

Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA)

Life-threatening complication of diabetes (especially Type 1) where lack of insulin causes the body to break down fat for fuel, producing ketones that acidify the blood. Symptoms: nausea, vomiting, confusion, fruity breath. Requires emergency treatment.

Biology

Ketones

Chemicals produced when the body breaks down fat for energy. Elevated blood ketones (ketonemia) indicate insulin deficiency and can lead to DKA. Abbott's upcoming dual-analyte sensor will monitor both glucose and beta-hydroxybutyrate (the primary blood ketone).

Disease

Atrial Fibrillation (AFib)

Most common heart arrhythmia. The atria (upper chambers) beat irregularly and rapidly due to disorganized electrical signals. Increases stroke risk 5x. Affects ~6 million Americans. Treated with medications, cardioversion, or catheter ablation.

Technology

Pulsed Field Ablation (PFA)

Next-generation catheter ablation technology using high-voltage, ultra-short electrical pulses to destroy cardiac tissue causing arrhythmia. Tissue-selective for cardiomyocytes — spares nerves, esophagus, and vasculature (unlike thermal ablation).

Biology

Electroporation

Process by which electrical pulses create pores in cell membranes. Reversible electroporation: temporary pores (used in gene therapy). Irreversible electroporation: permanent pores causing cell death (used in PFA ablation).

Technology

Radiofrequency Ablation (RFA)

Traditional catheter ablation method using heat from radiofrequency energy to destroy tissue. Effective but can damage non-target structures (esophagus, phrenic nerve). PFA aims to address these limitations.

Biology

Cardiomyocyte

Heart muscle cell. PFA is tissue-selective because cardiomyocytes are more susceptible to electroporation at lower energy thresholds than surrounding tissues (nerves, smooth muscle, esophageal cells).

Disease

Mitral Regurgitation (MR)

Condition where the mitral valve (between left atrium and left ventricle) doesn't close properly, allowing blood to flow backward. Primary MR: structural valve damage. Secondary/functional MR: ventricle dilation pulls leaflets apart. Treated by MitraClip.

Disease

Tricuspid Regurgitation (TR)

Backward blood flow through the tricuspid valve (right side of heart). Often secondary to left-heart disease or pulmonary hypertension. Treated by TriClip — the first-of-its-kind transcatheter therapy.

Technology

Transcatheter Edge-to-Edge Repair (TEER)

Minimally invasive technique where a clip is delivered via catheter to grasp and approximate valve leaflets, reducing regurgitation without open-heart surgery. Used for both mitral (MitraClip) and tricuspid (TriClip) repair.

Technology

TAVR (Transcatheter Aortic Valve Replacement)

Minimally invasive procedure to replace a diseased aortic valve with a prosthetic valve delivered via catheter (usually through the femoral artery). Alternative to open-heart surgical valve replacement.

Anatomy

Aortic Valve

Heart valve between the left ventricle and the aorta. When it becomes stenotic (narrowed) or regurgitant, blood flow is impaired. Aortic stenosis is the most common valve disease requiring intervention in elderly patients.

Technology

LAAC (Left Atrial Appendage Closure)

Procedure to seal off the left atrial appendage — a small pouch where blood clots tend to form in AFib patients. Eliminates the need for long-term blood thinners (anticoagulants) for stroke prevention.

Disease

Necrotizing Enterocolitis (NEC)

Life-threatening intestinal disease primarily affecting premature infants. The intestinal wall becomes inflamed and can die. Symptoms: feeding intolerance, abdominal distension, bloody stool. Mortality rate: 20–30%. Central to the Similac/Enfamil litigation.

Disease

Colorectal Cancer (CRC)

Cancer of the colon or rectum. Third most common cancer worldwide. Screening (colonoscopy or non-invasive tests like Cologuard) dramatically improves outcomes through early detection. USPSTF recommends screening starting at age 45.

Clinical

Paroxysmal AFib

Atrial fibrillation that comes and goes — episodes typically last less than 7 days and resolve on their own. Earlier stage of AFib progression. Volt PFA is approved for this indication.

Clinical

Persistent AFib

Atrial fibrillation episodes lasting longer than 7 days, or requiring intervention (cardioversion or ablation) to restore normal rhythm. Volt PFA is also approved for this indication.

Disease

Hypothyroidism

Underactive thyroid — insufficient production of thyroid hormones T3 and T4. Symptoms: fatigue, weight gain, cold intolerance, depression. Treated with levothyroxine (Synthroid). Affects ~5% of the global population.

Biology

Thyroxine (T4)

Thyroid hormone produced by the thyroid gland. Contains 4 iodine atoms. Converted peripherally to T3 (triiodothyronine), the more active form. Regulates metabolism, growth, and development.

Disease

Exocrine Pancreatic Insufficiency (EPI)

Condition where the pancreas fails to produce enough digestive enzymes. Causes maldigestion and malabsorption of fats, proteins, and carbohydrates. Common in cystic fibrosis and chronic pancreatitis. Treated with Creon (PERT).

Disease

Cystic Fibrosis (CF)

Genetic disease caused by mutations in the CFTR gene. Produces thick, sticky mucus that clogs airways and blocks pancreatic ducts. Most CF patients require PERT (Creon) for digestion.

Legal / Sales

MDL (Multidistrict Litigation)

A federal legal procedure that consolidates similar lawsuits from multiple districts into one court for pretrial proceedings. MDL 3026 consolidates NEC baby formula lawsuits in the Northern District of Illinois.

Pharma / Sales

Biosimilar

A biologic product that is highly similar to an already-approved reference biologic with no clinically meaningful differences. Unlike generic small-molecule drugs, biosimilars require clinical trials to demonstrate similarity. Growing rapidly as biologic patents expire.

Biology

Monoclonal Antibody (mAb)

Laboratory-made protein that mimics the immune system's ability to target specific antigens. Used in cancer, autoimmune disease, and more. Biosimilar mAbs are a key growth area for Abbott's EPD segment.

Biology

mAb (see Monoclonal Antibody)

Abbreviation for monoclonal antibody. These large biologic molecules are produced in cell culture (often CHO cells) and require complex manufacturing.

Biology

CHO Cells (Chinese Hamster Ovary)

The most commonly used mammalian host cells for large-scale production of therapeutic monoclonal antibodies and other biologic drugs. They add human-like post-translational modifications to proteins.

Clinical / Sales

AGP (Ambulatory Glucose Profile)

Standardized report generated from CGM data showing glucose patterns over time. Displays time-in-range, time above/below target, and variability. Physicians use AGP to adjust treatment — saves consultation time vs. reviewing raw glucose logs.

Technology

AID (Automated Insulin Delivery)

Closed-loop systems that automatically adjust insulin pump delivery based on real-time CGM data. Abbott is partnering to integrate Libre with multiple AID pump systems.

Pharma

Therapeutic Index (TI)

Ratio between the toxic dose and the therapeutic dose of a drug. Narrow TI drugs (like levothyroxine, warfarin, lithium) require precise dosing — small changes cause significant clinical effects. Formulation consistency is critical for generic substitution.

Drug Class

NSAID (Non-Steroidal Anti-Inflammatory Drug)

Class of drugs that reduce inflammation, pain, and fever by inhibiting cyclooxygenase (COX) enzymes. Includes ibuprofen (Brufen), naproxen, aspirin. Common side effects: GI irritation, cardiovascular risk with long-term use.

Biology

Cyclooxygenase (COX-1/COX-2)

Enzymes that convert arachidonic acid into prostaglandins. COX-1 is constitutive (protective — gut lining, platelets). COX-2 is inducible (inflammation). NSAIDs block both; selective COX-2 inhibitors (celecoxib) target only COX-2.

Biology

Prostaglandin

Lipid compounds with hormone-like effects. Mediate inflammation, pain, fever, and protect the gastric mucosa. Produced from arachidonic acid via COX enzymes. NSAIDs work by reducing prostaglandin synthesis.

Chemistry

Propionic Acid Derivative

Chemical class of NSAIDs including ibuprofen, naproxen, and ketoprofen. Named for their propionic acid backbone structure.

Drug Class

Fibrate

Class of lipid-lowering drugs that activate PPARα receptors. Primarily reduce triglycerides and raise HDL. Includes fenofibrate (Lipanthyl) and gemfibrozil.

Biology

PPARα (Peroxisome Proliferator-Activated Receptor Alpha)

Nuclear receptor that regulates fatty acid oxidation and lipoprotein metabolism. When activated by fibrates, increases lipoprotein lipase expression and reduces triglyceride production.

Biology

Triglycerides

The main form of fat in the body and in food. High levels (>150 mg/dL) increase cardiovascular risk. Reduced by fibrates, omega-3s, statins, and lifestyle changes.

Biology

HDL Cholesterol

"Good" cholesterol — high-density lipoprotein. Carries cholesterol away from arteries to the liver for removal. Higher levels are associated with lower cardiovascular risk. Fibrates raise HDL.

Biology

Lipoprotein Lipase

Enzyme that breaks down triglycerides in lipoproteins (like VLDL). Activated by PPARα agonists (fibrates). Increased activity = lower triglycerides.

Drug Class

Statin

Class of drugs that lower LDL cholesterol by inhibiting HMG-CoA reductase (the rate-limiting enzyme in cholesterol synthesis). First-line therapy for dyslipidemia. Fibrates used adjunctively for elevated triglycerides.

Disease

Dyslipidemia

Abnormal levels of lipids (cholesterol, triglycerides) in the blood. Major risk factor for cardiovascular disease. Treated with statins (LDL), fibrates (triglycerides), and lifestyle modification.

Enzyme

Lipase

Digestive enzyme that breaks down dietary fats (triglycerides) into glycerol and fatty acids. Key component of Creon (PERT) for patients with EPI.

Enzyme

Protease

Digestive enzyme that breaks down proteins into amino acids. Component of Creon (PERT).

Enzyme

Amylase

Digestive enzyme that breaks down starch into sugars. Component of Creon (PERT). Also produced by salivary glands.

Therapy

PERT (Pancreatic Enzyme Replacement Therapy)

Oral enzyme supplements taken with meals to replace missing pancreatic enzymes in EPI patients. Dosed based on lipase units per meal. Patients take PERT with every meal for life.

Biology

Short-Chain Fatty Acids (SCFAs)

Metabolic byproducts of bacterial fermentation of fiber/lactulose in the colon. Include acetate, propionate, butyrate. Lower colonic pH (which reduces ammonia absorption in hepatic encephalopathy) and draw water into the bowel.

Disease

Hepatic Encephalopathy

Brain dysfunction caused by liver failure's inability to clear toxins (especially ammonia) from the blood. Symptoms range from confusion to coma. Treated with lactulose (Duphalac) and rifaximin.

Biology

Casein

The primary protein in cow's milk (~80% of total milk protein). Forms curds during digestion. Used as the protein base in many infant formulas.

Biology

Whey

The liquid portion of milk after curdling (~20% of total milk protein). Contains lactalbumin and lactoglobulin. More easily digestible than casein. Used in infant formulas and nutritional supplements.

Biology

HMOs (Human Milk Oligosaccharides)

Complex carbohydrates naturally found in breast milk that serve as prebiotics (feed beneficial gut bacteria) and support immune development. Abbott's Similac Pro-Advance includes 2'-FL HMO.

Biology

Prebiotic

Non-digestible dietary compounds (like HMOs, inulin, FOS) that selectively promote growth of beneficial gut bacteria. Different from probiotics (live bacteria).

Nutrient

Lutein

Carotenoid antioxidant important for eye health. Found in breast milk. Added to Similac formulas. Helps filter blue light and protect the developing retina.

Vitamin

Vitamin E

Fat-soluble antioxidant that protects cell membranes from oxidative damage. Important for infant brain development. Added to infant formulas.

Chemistry

Hydrolyzed Protein

Protein that has been broken down into smaller peptides or amino acids using enzymes or acid. Extensively hydrolyzed formulas (like Similac Alimentum) are used for infants with cow's milk protein allergy — the smaller peptides are less likely to trigger immune reactions.

Biology

Soy Protein Isolate

Highly refined form of soybean protein used in nutritional supplements (Ensure). Contains all essential amino acids. Plant-based alternative to dairy protein.

Nutrition

Slow-Release Carbohydrate

Modified carbohydrate blend (e.g., modified maltodextrin, fructose) designed to be digested and absorbed more slowly, producing a lower glycemic response. Used in Glucerna for diabetes management.

Chemistry

Fructose

Simple sugar (monosaccharide, C₆H₁₂O₆) found in fruit. Has a lower glycemic index than glucose. Used in Glucerna's slow-release carbohydrate blend.

Biology

Glycemic (Impact/Index)

Refers to the effect of food on blood glucose levels. Glycemic index (GI) ranks foods by how quickly they raise blood sugar. Low-GI foods are preferred for diabetes management.

Biology

Electrolytes

Minerals (sodium, potassium, chloride, etc.) that carry electrical charge in body fluids. Essential for nerve function, muscle contraction, and fluid balance. Pedialyte provides optimal electrolyte balance for rehydration.

Clinical

ORS (Oral Rehydration Salt)

WHO-recommended formulation of salts and glucose for treating dehydration. Principle: glucose facilitates sodium and water absorption in the small intestine. Pedialyte is based on ORS principles.

Drug Class

Anticoagulant

Blood-thinning medication (warfarin, DOACs like apixaban/rivaroxaban) used to prevent stroke in AFib. Some patients can't tolerate anticoagulants (bleeding risk), making them candidates for LAAC (Amplatzer Amulet).

Technology

SCS (Spinal Cord Stimulation)

Neuromodulation therapy where an implanted device delivers electrical impulses to the spinal cord, interrupting pain signals. Used for chronic back/leg pain, CRPS, and diabetic neuropathy.

Biology / Technology

DRG (Dorsal Root Ganglion)

Cluster of sensory nerve cell bodies along the spinal cord. DRG stimulation is a targeted form of neuromodulation for localized pain conditions.

Diagnostics

Immunoassay

Biochemical test that uses antibody-antigen binding to measure the concentration of a substance (hormone, protein, drug) in blood or other samples. Abbott's Alinity platforms run automated immunoassays.

Chemistry

Chemiluminescence

Emission of light as a result of a chemical reaction. Used in diagnostic immunoassays: a luminescent label bound to the analyte produces light proportional to the analyte concentration when triggered by a chemical reagent.

Biology

Antigen

Any substance (typically a protein) that triggers an immune response. In rapid diagnostic tests (BinaxNOW), viral antigens are detected using antibody-coated test strips.

Technology

Immunochromatographic Assay

Lateral flow test technology (like BinaxNOW). Sample migrates along a membrane strip. If the target antigen is present, it binds to labeled antibodies and produces a visible line at the test zone.

Biology

DNA (Deoxyribonucleic Acid)

Molecule carrying genetic instructions. Cologuard detects methylated DNA biomarkers shed by colorectal tumors into stool. Altered methylation patterns are cancer indicators.

Biology

Hemoglobin

Oxygen-carrying protein in red blood cells. Cologuard also tests for hemoglobin in stool (occult blood), which can indicate colorectal bleeding from tumors or polyps.

Technology

PCR (Polymerase Chain Reaction)

Laboratory technique that amplifies small segments of DNA millions of times for detection. Used in Cologuard to detect cancer-associated DNA biomarkers. Also used in molecular diagnostics (COVID testing).

Technology

RT-PCR (Reverse Transcription PCR)

Variant of PCR that first converts RNA to DNA (reverse transcription) before amplification. Used in Oncotype DX to measure gene expression levels in tumor tissue.

Lab

FFPE (Formalin-Fixed Paraffin-Embedded)

Standard method of preserving tissue samples. Tissue is fixed in formalin, embedded in paraffin wax, then sliced for analysis. Oncotype DX analyzes gene expression from FFPE breast tumor samples.

Technology

MCED (Multi-Cancer Early Detection)

Blood-based tests that screen for multiple types of cancer simultaneously by detecting circulating tumor DNA (ctDNA) or other biomarkers. CancerGuard (Exact Sciences) is Abbott's entry in this emerging space.

Clinical

MRD (Molecular Residual Disease)

Detection of tiny amounts of remaining cancer after treatment using molecular methods. OncoDetect (Exact Sciences) monitors for MRD and recurrence risk using ctDNA analysis.
