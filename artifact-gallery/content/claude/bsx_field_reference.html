FARAPULSE PFA System

FlagshipEP · US market leader

The single biggest growth driver in the company. FARAPULSE treats atrial fibrillation using pulsed field ablationPulsed Field Ablation (PFA)Non-thermal ablation that uses short, high-voltage electrical pulses to destroy heart tissue via irreversible electroporation, selectively sparing nearby structures. rather than heat or cold. What prescribers actually care about
  • Safety margin: tissue selectivity reduces esophageal and phrenic-nerve injury risk vs. thermal — the #1 adoption driver cited by physicians.
  • Procedural efficiency / throughput: faster case times increase lab volume — a direct economic argument to the hospital, not just the operator.
  • Durability: ADVENT 4-year data showed greater long-term AFib success, fewer arrhythmia hospitalizations, lower repeat-ablation rates vs. thermal.
  • Label breadth: now approved for both paroxysmal and persistent AFib (incl. posterior-wall + pulmonary-vein), ~doubling the treatable population.
  • Ecosystem lock-in counter: J&J's CARTO integration is the competitive threat — OPAL HDx is the answer to "but it's not on my map."
TAM & quality-of-life impact

BSX frames the global EP / AFib-ablation market at roughly $16B and growing fast; persistent AFib alone is estimated at a potential ~$1B opportunity by 2028. Clinically, successful ablation can restore normal rhythm, reduce stroke/heart-failure risk, and meaningfully improve symptom burden and exercise tolerance. Probability-of-success framing: high — FARAPULSE is approved, market-leading in the US, and backed by long-term RCT data. The risk is share, not viability.

What prescribers care about (and the CHAMPION-AF inflection)
  • Adherence problem: ~40% of AFib patients on blood thinners don't take them consistently — the core clinical pitch.
  • CHAMPION-AF (March 2026): met all endpoints — statistically superior bleeding protection and similar efficacy vs. NOACs in a broad NVAF population. This is the data that supports a potential first-line expansion beyond today's bleeding-risk-restricted label.
  • One-and-done: single implant vs. lifetime drug cost and bleeding risk — strong patient and payer narrative.
  • Concomitant workflow: pairing with ablation lowers long-term stroke risk in one trip to the lab.
Compliance flag: the CHAMPION-AF first-line use is investigational until label expansion is granted. Reps must not promote first-line positioning off the current bleeding-risk-restricted indication. Verify the live label before any first-line talk track.

The largest MedSurg unit and a durable double-digit grower, built on high-recurring single-use disposables.   • Single-use disposables = predictable recurring revenue and high revenue visibility for the hospital's GI service line.

Urology — Rezūm, Stone Management, SNM

Mixed momentum

Rezūm uses water-vapor (steam) thermal energy to ablate obstructing prostate tissue in BPHBPHBenign Prostatic Hyperplasia — non-cancerous prostate enlargement causing urinary obstruction; very high prevalence in aging men., and gained an expanded FDA indication for larger glands. Stone management runs on the LithoVue single-use scope and Lumenis/Moses holmium lasers. Sacral neuromodulation (Axonics) and tibial-nerve stim (eCoin/Valencia) treat overactive bladder and incontinence.

Prescriber sales points — Rezūm
  • QoL preservation: Rezūm preserves sexual function vs. surgical resection (TURPTURPTransurethral Resection of the Prostate — the surgical gold-standard for BPH; effective but carries higher sexual-dysfunction risk and longer recovery.) — the central patient conversation.
  • Office-based: minimally invasive, can be done in-office, fast recovery — economic upside for urology practices.
  • Substitutes: TURP, UroLift (Teleflex), GreenLight laser, and the drug pathway (alpha-blockers / 5-ARIs).
Q1 2026: management explicitly flagged urology as below expectations (+2.1%), citing weakness in the core business and in neuromodulation, while expecting sacral neuromodulation (Axonics) to improve over time. Don't over-promise near-term urology momentum in manager-facing materials.

Scary headwinds & exciting tailwinds

The honest read

The candid view a manager will want — what could go wrong, and what's genuinely working. Severity/strength tags are judgment calls flagged for discussion.

▼Headwinds

High
PFA competition is intensifying
J&J's VARIPULSE plugs into the dominant CARTO mapping platform, creating switching costs; Medtronic's Affera/Sphere-9 is mapping-integrated too. The threat is share erosion, not market viability.
High
2026 guidance was cut
After the Q1 2026 call, management lowered full-year organic growth guidance from a ~10–11% range to ~6.5–8.0%, citing unanticipated headwinds in electrophysiology and Watchman volumes. This reframes the hyper-growth thesis.
Medium
Urology underperformance
Q1 2026 urology grew just ~2.1%; management expects a "below-market year" in urology and softness in core neuromodulation within the unit.
Medium
Structural-heart strategic gap (TAVR exit)
BSX discontinued the Acurate neo2 / Acurate Prime TAVR systems globally in May 2025 after the ACURATE IDE trial failed its non-inferiority margin vs. Medtronic Evolut and Edwards Sapien. ~$200M of 2024 sales gone, and no TAVR franchise — a notable hole in structural heart.
Medium
FX & tariff exposure
~40% international sales = real currency drag in strong-dollar periods; management has cited a ~$200M tariff impact.
Medium
Integration & leverage risk
The ~$14.5B Penumbra deal is funded largely with ~$11B of cash/new debt and must clear regulatory review and integrate a neurovascular business BSX exited in 2011.

▲Tailwinds

FARAPULSE is the US PFA leader
