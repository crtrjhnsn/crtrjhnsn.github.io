ABusiness-Model Snapshot

What Lilly sells, in plain English. Lilly discovers, manufactures, and sells patent-protected prescription medicines. The business today is overwhelmingly a single-molecule story: tirzepatide — sold as Mounjaro approved for type-2 diabetes and Zepbound approved for obesity — drove 56% of 2025 revenue1. Around that core sit an oncology franchise (Verzenio), immunology (Taltz, Omvoh, Ebglyss), a maturing CNS asset (Kisunla in Alzheimer's), the legacy diabetes book (Trulicity, Jardiance), and — newest and most consequential — Foundayo (orforglipron) approved, the first oral GLP-1 pill cleared in the U.S. that can be taken any time of day with no food or water restrictions1.

Core customer segments

  • Patients — increasingly self-paying: ~45% of Zepbound scripts and ~55% of new scripts were cash-pay in Q1 20267.
  • Prescribers / specialists & KOLs — endocrinologists, PCPs, oncologists; demand created via medical affairs and DTC advertising (an expense, not a channel).
  • Payers / PBMs / national systems — set formulary access and extract gross-to-net rebates.
  • Wholesalers & specialty pharmacies — the booking point of sale; plus LillyDirect, Lilly's own telehealth/pharmacy channel.
  • Government tender buyers — ex-U.S. health systems; and now U.S. Medicare/Medicaid under the 2025–26 pricing agreement6.

How the company makes money

  • On-patent premium pricing net of large rebates — gross margin ~83%3.
  • Volume-led indication expansion — diabetes → obesity → sleep apnea → CV/MASH adjacencies.
  • New launches (Foundayo, Kisunla, Ebglyss, Omvoh, Jaypirca, Inluriyo).
  • Geographic rollout — ex-U.S. volume +95% in Q1 20267.
  • Operating leverage — non-GAAP performance margin hit ~50% in Q1 202610.
  • Harvest of legacy diabetes (Trulicity, Jardiance via the Boehringer Ingelheim collaboration).
The 5 KPIs that summarize the model
  1. Incretin franchise revenue & YoY growth (Mounjaro + Zepbound + orforglipron) — the whole thesis.
  2. Volume vs. realized-price split — 2026 is +65% volume / −13% price; the durability of demand against price give-back is the crux.
  3. Gross margin % — pricing pressure vs. mix/cost-of-production improvement.
  4. Performance margin — operating leverage as launches scale.
  5. Phase-3 pipeline conversion — replenishment ahead of the mid-2030s tirzepatide patent cliff.

BMoney-Flow Map

Pharma cash conversion runs in a long loop: capital is committed to manufacturing months to years before the matching revenue exists. The diagram traces a dollar from discovery to cash, and flags where margin risk is created.

Three points an IC should internalize:

  • Revenue is booked at the wholesaler/specialty-pharmacy/tender hand-off, already net of gross-to-net deductions. There is no "DTC channel" — U.S. consumer advertising is demand-generation opex, not a sales line.
  • Reported net-sales growth ≠ volume growth. In 2025, worldwide volume rose far faster than reported revenue because Lilly is deliberately giving back price (rebates + list cuts) to widen access. The 2026 split — +65% volume, −13% price5 — is the single most important decomposition in the model.
  • Build-ahead is the hidden risk. Lilly commits bioreactor and fill-finish capacity, and now oral-pill capacity, against a demand forecast. If demand softens, that capex converts into under-absorption and potential inventory write-offs — margin damage that shows up quarters after the manufacturing decision.

DBrand, Product & Franchise Strategy

Why prevalence and indication breadth are the economics. A molecule's value is roughly (eligible population) × (treatment rate) × (net price) × (persistence) × (years of exclusivity). Lilly's incretin platform scores high on every term: obesity and T2D are enormous, under-treated populations; net price — even after give-back — remains premium; persistence is structurally high for chronic metabolic disease; and exclusivity runs to the mid-2030s. Indication breadth multiplies the population term without re-paying most of the discovery cost: the same molecule is monetized across diabetes, obesity, sleep apnea, and (investigational) cardiovascular, MASH and osteoarthritis adjacencies.

The product engine — strengths

  • Best-in-class efficacy. Tirzepatide's dual mechanism delivered weight loss "unprecedented for a single agent" and out-performed semaglutide 1.0mg on HbA1c and weight in head-to-head data.12
  • Innovation cadence. >25 positive Phase-3 topline readouts in 2025; 12 new Phase-3 starts.4
  • Format innovation. Oral orforglipron breaks the injection/cold-chain ceiling on the category.

The product engine — risks

  • Concentration. One molecule = 56% of revenue. A tirzepatide safety signal or faster-than-modeled cliff is the dominant tail risk.
  • Trial / discontinuation risk. Pipeline misses (e.g. CV-outcomes, MASH) would re-rate the durability story.
  • Competitive crowding. Novo's semaglutide/CagriSema, plus Amgen MariTide, Viking, Pfizer oral programs.

Exclusivity, cliffs & demand creation. Patent-protected scarcity is the pricing-power source, but it is time-boxed. Lilly's durable moat is therefore the execution machine — discovery → trials → manufacturing scale-up → KOL/medical-affairs demand creation → launch — repeated faster than rivals, plus indication expansion as a moderate life-cycle extender. Per-molecule, the moat decays to the patent clock; as a franchise, it is replenished by the pipeline.

EDrug-Development, Manufacturing & Channel Economics

From raw material to dose. Tirzepatide is an acylated peptide — biologically manufactured and filled/finished into injectable pens under cold-chain, a capital-intensive, long-lead process. Orforglipron is a small molecule taken orally — chemically synthesized, far cheaper to make and ship, and not constrained by injectable fill-finish or the same cold-chain. That manufacturing difference is a strategic weapon: an oral GLP-1 can be scaled to a global population in a way injectables structurally cannot.

Customer acquisition

Cost-per-new-patient-start is driven by DTC + sales-force + medical affairs. LillyDirect lowers friction (direct telehealth → fulfillment) and captures first-party demand data.

Repeat / persistence

Chronic dosing = recurring revenue, but real-world discontinuation (cost, tolerability, supply) is the leak. Persistence is the obesity-category "units-per-transaction."

Fulfillment & liability

Cold-chain + specialty-pharmacy cost for injectables; recall/return and product-liability litigation are the tail. Compounded-GLP-1 grey market is a quality & brand risk.

In-house manufacturing vs. partnership reach. They are mutually reinforcing, not substitutes. Lilly is spending aggressively on owned capacity (multiple new U.S. and ex-U.S. plants) to control supply — the binding constraint on incretin revenue in 2023–24. Where it partners — most notably the Boehringer Ingelheim diabetes alliance (Jardiance/empagliflozin) — it trades a slice of margin for scale and de-risking; this shows up as collaboration revenue / other income rather than full product sales. The 2025–26 wave of bolt-on acquisitions (Verve, Scorpion, Orna, Centessa, Kelonia, Ajax) buys pipeline optionality and modality breadth (RNA, gene editing, targeted protein degradation).9

Best-available productivity proxies: plant productivity → gross-margin trend & qualitative utilization commentary (Lilly does not disclose plant-level utilization); commercial profitability → SG&A/sales and the performance-margin trend (~43% → ~50% YoY in Q1 2026).10

Volume vs. price — how to read it

Reported growth = volume × price/mix. Lilly's 2026 print of +56% revenue from +65% volume and −13% price is the healthy pattern: it is share/access capture, not erosion. The dangerous version is the inverse — revenue held up by price while volume rolls over. Watch the gap between the two every quarter.

Healthy vs. dangerous inventory

Healthy build-ahead

Pre-launch & capacity inventory built against confirmed demand and approvals; Lilly capitalizes some pre-launch inventory when commercialization is probable. Rising inventory alongside rising sales = supplying real demand.

Dangerous inventory

Inventory growth outrunning sales growth; perishable/seasonal stock; capacity committed to a demand curve that flattens → write-offs + under-absorption. Track inventory-turns and the inventory-vs-sales growth gap.

How a slowdown propagates: demand softens → built capacity under-absorbs (fixed cost spread over fewer units) → gross margin compresses → finished/WIP inventory ages → write-offs → working capital ties up cash → free cash flow falls — typically 1–3 quarters after the demand inflection.

GInternational Expansion

Why it matters. Ex-U.S. growth (1) diversifies away from U.S. pricing/policy concentration (IRA, PBMs, Medicaid), and (2) extends each asset's economic life by rolling the same molecule across new geographies and reimbursement regimes. Ex-U.S. was +81% in Q1 2026 on +95% volume, and Lilly holds >53% of the ex-U.S. incretin market7 — with much of Europe/China/Brazil demand paid out of pocket.3

What must be true to be durable

  • Disease awareness & diagnosis rates rise.
  • Reimbursement / HTA access (or sustained cash-pay willingness).
  • Local manufacturing & cold-chain to serve volume.
  • Prescriber/specialist reach & tender timing execution.

Risks

  • Local biosimilar/generic champions (esp. China post-LOE; India).
  • Health-budget/fiscal softness; China VBP price pressure.
  • Geopolitics & pharma tariffs; FX.
  • Launch-cost drag / over-expansion if uptake lags.

IPractical Modeling Blueprint

Driver-based architecture

  • Revenue = Σ (product net sales) = volume × net price, modeled by franchise & geography.
  • Gross margin = portfolio/product margin − gross-to-net deductions ± FX/tariffs/freight ± manufacturing absorption.
  • Opex = sales force + medical affairs + marketing/DTC + G&A + R&D + distribution.
  • Below gross profit: collaboration profit-share (e.g. Boehringer) in other income/expense; acquired IPR&D charges are lumpy and distort GAAP.
  • Operating income → non-GAAP net income / EPS (tax ~16–17% non-GAAP; buyback-adjusted share count ~910M and falling).
  • FCF = operating cash − Δworking capital (watch inventory) − capex − leases → funds dividend + buyback.

KKey-Drug Deep Dive — Tirzepatide & Orforglipron

Tirzepatide (Mounjaro / Zepbound) approved

How it works in the body

Tirzepatide is an acylated peptide engineered to activate two receptors at once — the GIP and GLP-1 incretin receptors. Both receptors sit on pancreatic β-cells (where they boost glucose-dependent insulin secretion — i.e. insulin rises mainly when blood sugar is high, limiting hypoglycemia risk) and in brain regions that govern satiety, reducing appetite and food intake. The two incretins diverge on glucagon (GIP raises it during low glucose; GLP-1 suppresses it during high glucose) and on fat handling (GIP supports lipogenesis; GLP-1 indirectly promotes lipolysis), which together improve adipose health. According to PubMed, this combination underlies the metabolic benefit.13

Administration & formulation

Once-weekly subcutaneous injection via single-dose pen; biologically/peptide-manufactured with fill-finish + cold-chain. Titrated 5–15 mg/week. Formulation consequence: the injectable + cold-chain requirement is exactly the bottleneck that the oral orforglipron is designed to bypass.

Disease targeted

Type-2 diabetes (insulin resistance + inadequate insulin → chronic high blood glucose) and obesity (chronic excess adiposity driving metabolic, CV and mechanical disease), plus obstructive sleep apnea.

Efficacy & safety vs prior therapies (from the literature)

Across the SURPASS 1–5 program, tirzepatide 5–15 mg/week reduced HbA1c by ~1.2–2.6 percentage points and body weight by ~5–12 kg — magnitudes the literature describes as unprecedented for a single agent — and out-performed semaglutide 1.0 mg on both HbA1c and weight, with cardiovascular safety supported across the program (MACE-4). Key adverse events are gastrointestinal — nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, constipation — and are dose-dependent, the same class profile as selective GLP-1 agonists. According to PubMed.12 The differentiation vs earlier diabetes drugs is the dual mechanism delivering combined glycemic + weight efficacy in one molecule.

Orforglipron (Foundayo) approved (obesity) T2D filed

Orforglipron is a non-peptide, small-molecule, oral GLP-1 receptor agonist — the first oral GLP-1 the U.S. FDA has approved that can be taken any time of day without food or water restrictions.1 Competitive significance: as a synthesized small molecule it sidesteps the injectable peptide's cold-chain and fill-finish constraints, so it is far cheaper and faster to scale — the single most important lever for reaching the global obesity/diabetes population and for defending share against Novo's oral semaglutide. The trade-off vs injectable tirzepatide is generally somewhat lower peak efficacy, exchanged for convenience, manufacturability and access.
