A rough draft — ready for your edits

The company behind biology at scale.

Amgen is a large-cap biotech with four declared therapeutic areas — General Medicine, Oncology, Inflammation, and Rare Disease — plus a deliberate biosimilar franchise that competes on reference biologics. The story of 2026 is a company straddling two eras: defending a base eroding to loss-of-exclusivity (LOE) and Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) price setting on the one hand, while repositioning around MariTide in obesity, a fast-growing rare-disease bench from the Horizon acquisition, and a first-in-class oncology BiTE on the other.

Step 3 · The brand-name book

Innovative portfolio — what each drug actually is

These are Amgen-originated (or Amgen-owned post-Horizon) brands, not biosimilars. For each drug I'll flag the mechanism, the chemistry, how it's administered, the payer lever, the total addressable market (TAM), and where it sits on the LOE clock.

MariTide

maridebart cafraglutide · Phase 3
Amgen's swing at the obesity category.

A long-acting peptide-antibody conjugate: a monoclonal antibody that blocks the GIP receptor fused to two GLP-1 receptor agonist peptides via amino-acid linkers. That dual mechanism — GLP-1 activation plus GIP antagonism — was Amgen's human-genetics bet that blocking GIP (rather than activating it, as Zepbound does) might yield more durable weight loss. Half-life is roughly 21 days, which is what allows monthly (or less frequent) dosing.

Target indications
Obesity, T2D, heart failure, OSA, ASCVD
Phase 2 result
Up to ~20% mean weight loss at 52 wks; up to ~17% in obesity + T2D; HbA1c dropped up to 2.2 pp (NEJM, Jun 2025)
Administration
Subcutaneous autoinjector, monthly
TAM
Global anti-obesity medication market forecast ~$95B by 2030 (Goldman); GLP-1 class ~$190B by 2035 (Morgan Stanley)
Readout timing

Repatha

evolocumab
The PCSK9 workhorse, now with primary-prevention data.

A fully human IgG2 monoclonal antibody against PCSK9. By binding circulating PCSK9, it prevents the enzyme from degrading LDL receptors, so the liver pulls more LDL-C out of the bloodstream. The VESALIUS-CV Phase 3 study reported in 2025 that Repatha cut first MACE by 25% and first heart attack by 36% in high-risk patients with no prior MI or stroke — that is the primary-prevention story Amgen is now selling.

Chemistry
IgG2 monoclonal antibody, produced via CHO cell culture
Administration
SC autoinjector: 140 mg every 2 wks or 420 mg monthly
TAM
Tens of millions of US adults with ASCVD not at LDL goal; $3.0B in Amgen sales FY2025
LOE
US patents running ~2029–2030; EU earlier in 2028
Sales growth
Double-digit in recent quarters; primary-prevention data is the catalyst

Prolia / XGEVA

denosumab
Same molecule, two books, one biosimilar cliff.

A fully human monoclonal antibody against RANKL. Binding RANKL stops osteoclasts from forming and resorbing bone. Prolia is the osteoporosis dose (60 mg SC every 6 months); XGEVA is the higher-dose oncology indication for bone metastases and giant-cell tumor.

EVENITY

romosozumab-aqqg
Bone-building, not just bone-preserving.

A humanized monoclonal antibody that binds sclerostin, which ordinarily slows bone formation. Blocking it produces a simultaneous anabolic (build) and anti-resorptive effect — unusual among osteoporosis drugs. Delivered as two SC injections once monthly for 12 doses.

A bispecific T-cell engager (BiTE) that bridges DLL3 on SCLC cells to CD3 on cytotoxic T cells, forcing a tumor-directed T-cell attack. Full FDA approval in 2025 for 2L+ ES-SCLC with overall-survival benefit vs chemo.

TEZSPIRE

Anti-TSLP biologic for severe asthma — now nasal polyps.

A human monoclonal antibody that binds TSLP, an epithelial alarmin sitting upstream of multiple inflammatory cascades. Unlike Type-2 biologics, TEZSPIRE works across eosinophilic and non-eosinophilic asthma phenotypes, which is the core selling point. Otezla

apremilast
Oral plaque psoriasis / PsA — now an IRA casualty.

A small-molecule PDE4 inhibitor taken as a BID oral tablet — no injections, no lab monitoring. That convenience has been its selling point in milder patients. Selected for Medicare price setting under the IRA, which triggered a $1.2B intangible-asset impairment at Amgen in 2025.

Enbrel

etanercept
The TNF inhibitor that just keeps running.

A fusion protein combining the extracellular portion of the TNF receptor with an IgG1 Fc region. It acts as a decoy receptor that sponges up TNF-α, dampening immune-mediated inflammation in psoriasis, RA, PsA, ankylosing spondylitis.

BLINCYTO

blinatumomab
The BiTE that proved the platform.

Bispecific T-cell engager linking CD19 on B-cell precursors to CD3 on T cells, used in B-ALL. Delivered as continuous IV infusion. Expanding into frontline MRD-positive ALL settings has been a major growth lever — Q4 2025 sales were up 58% YoY.

TEPEZZA

teprotumumab-trbw
The TED franchise, acquired from Horizon.

A fully human monoclonal antibody against the IGF-1 receptor, which is overexpressed on orbital fibroblasts in thyroid eye disease. It's the only FDA-approved therapy for TED.

Administration
IV infusion every 3 wks × 8 doses
Notable safety

KRYSTEXXA

pegloticase
Last-line therapy for uncontrolled gout.

A PEGylated recombinant uricase enzyme that metabolizes uric acid directly to allantoin, collapsing serum urate in patients whose gout has failed xanthine-oxidase inhibitors. Administered IV every 2 weeks; frequently co-administered with methotrexate to reduce anti-drug antibody formation and improve durability.

TAM
~100,000 US patients with refractory tophaceous gout

UPLIZNA

inebilizumab-cdon
B-cell depleter with the widest new label of 2025–26.

A humanized anti-CD19 monoclonal antibody that depletes a broad range of B cells — including plasmablasts and some plasma cells — implicated in autoantibody-driven disease. Already approved in NMOSD; FDA added IgG4-RD and generalized myasthenia gravis (gMG) as indications in 2025; EC followed in Feb 2026 for gMG.

Administration
Two IV loading doses, then maintenance every 6 months
Q3 2025 sales
~$155M, up 46% YoY
TAM
NMOSD ~15,000 US; IgG4-RD thousands; gMG 56,000–123,000 Europe alone

TAVNEOS

avacopan
Oral complement pathway drug for ANCA vasculitis.

A small-molecule selective C5a receptor (C5aR1) antagonist, an adjunct for severe ANCA-associated vasculitis. Designed to reduce steroid exposure in a chronically steroid-dependent population. Sales up 84% YoY in Q4 2025.

Kyprolis

carfilzomib
Next-gen proteasome inhibitor in multiple myeloma.

An epoxyketone proteasome inhibitor that irreversibly binds the proteasome's chymotrypsin-like site, causing protein misfold buildup and myeloma cell apoptosis. Used in relapsed/refractory myeloma combinations.

Nplate

romiplostim
TPO mimetic for ITP and radiation exposure.

A peptibody (peptide + Fc) that activates the thrombopoietin receptor (MPL) to stimulate platelet production. Used in chronic ITP and as a medical countermeasure for hematopoietic injury from acute radiation syndrome.

Step 4 · Generic-plays-for-biologics

Biosimilars — what we're copying, and when

How a biosimilar actually wins a script

Biosimilars rarely win by persuading the doctor; they win by being on the right tier of the payer's formulary, and — if they carry an interchangeable designation — by giving the pharmacist legal cover to substitute without calling the prescriber. Amgen was the first company to launch a US Humira biosimilar (Jan 2023), and captured ~$466M in Amjevita sales in year one.

Step 6 · The legal docket

Ongoing lawsuits you should know exist

Every drug has a litigation footprint. Here's what's still live as of early 2026, organized by segment.

General Medicine / Bone Health

  • Denosumab multidistrict litigation (MDL No. 3138) — D.N.J.
    Consolidated BPCIA patent suits over Prolia/XGEVA biosimilars. Sandoz and Celltrion have settled (late-May 2025 launch pathway); other biosimilar developers remain in the MDL.

Inflammation / Immunology

  • Enbrel patent defenses
    Amgen successfully defended composition and method-of-use patents that keep Samsung Bioepis' Eticovo and Sandoz's Erelzi off the US market until 2029; Supreme Court declined cert in 2021.
  • Otezla IRA price-setting challenge context
    Amgen recorded a $1.2B non-cash impairment in 2025 tied to CMS selecting Otezla for Medicare negotiation. Industry-wide IRA challenges (various plaintiffs vs HHS) remain under appeal; Amgen has not been a headline plaintiff but the outcomes are directly financially material.

Corporate / Securities

  • Shareholder class action re: $10.7B IRS tax dispute
    Filed 2023 by a Michigan pension fund. In 2024 a SDNY judge denied Amgen's motion to dismiss; case proceeds. Allegations center on disclosure adequacy about an IRS dispute over 2010–2015 income allocation between the US and Puerto Rico.
  • IRS examinations
    Amgen's 10-Q disclosures confirm ongoing IRS exams for 2016–2018 on issues similar to the earlier period, with potential extension to later years. Resolution not expected within 12 months.

Oncology / Rare Disease

  • No headline Amgen-as-defendant litigation on IMDELLTRA, TEPEZZA, UPLIZNA, or TAVNEOS at this writing. Standard product-liability exposure applies across the rare-disease book inherited from Horizon.

Sales-desk guidance

Avoid discussing active litigation with customers. If a provider or institution raises a litigation question, route it to Medical Affairs / Legal. This page is for internal awareness only so you don't get blindsided in a hallway conversation.

Step 7 · The human in the loop

Consumer experience & medical-provider adoption

Uptake in pharma is rarely about the molecule — it's about how the molecule fits into a clinician's day and a patient's life. Here's the uptake shape by audience, and the points that tend to close the deal.

Uptake by prescriber segment

Prescriber Typical uptake pattern What they care about most
Primary care (FM / IM) Slow early; accelerates sharply once guideline inclusion + payer coverage line up (classic GLP-1 RA adoption curve) PA burden, titration simplicity, adherence tooling, time-to-approval, clear patient education
Cardiology Faster for drugs with CV-outcomes evidence (Repatha VESALIUS-CV); skeptical of surrogate-endpoint stories Hard endpoints (MACE, mortality), NNT, safety in polypharmacy
Endocrinology Early adopters for obesity & bone; comfortable with injectables; wary of novel mechanisms without outcome data Glycemic effect, durability post-discontinuation, body-comp data, GI adverse events
Oncology Adoption driven by NCCN inclusion and academic KOL enthusiasm; community uptake lags academic by 6–18 months Overall survival, PFS, toxicity management, reimbursement (J-code & ASP-based buy-and-bill)

Neurology / Ophthalmology (rare disease) Low raw prescriber count; each champion moves a disproportionate share Rarity-aware dosing, infusion center access, durability, post-marketing registries

Closing points doctors actually react to

  • Outcome data, not surrogates. Cardiologists prefer MACE-reduction over LDL-C percentages; oncologists prefer OS over PFS when available.
  • Low administrative burden. Prior-auth turnaround, free-drug bridge programs, nurse educators, and a clean specialty-pharmacy workflow.
  • Dosing interval that matches the patient's life. Once-monthly beats weekly. Once-every-six-months (UPLIZNA, Prolia) is almost always preferred for chronic maintenance.
  • Adherence data. Real-world persistence numbers beat trial numbers in the conversation.
  • Co-pay support. Manufacturer co-pay cards and foundation assistance programs are frequently what actually closes the prescription.
  • Phenotype-agnostic efficacy. TEZSPIRE's TSLP mechanism works across asthma phenotypes — that's a single-sentence differentiator.

Consumer (patient) experience drivers

  • Device quality. A pen or autoinjector that fires cleanly, with minimal site reactions, outperforms a vial-and-syringe experience.
  • Onboarding. Nurse-led first-injection education reduces discontinuation, especially for obesity and biologics.
  • Adverse-event experience. For GLP-1-class drugs, GI tolerability drives 15%+ of discontinuations (Goldman survey).
  • Cash exposure. For patients, the real question is out-of-pocket, not list price. Cost and loss of insurance coverage drive the majority of GLP-1 discontinuations (60–64% per recent survey data).

The new obesity access map

Under the CMS BALANCE model (announced December 2025), state Medicaid agencies can opt in from May 2026 through January 2027, and eligible Part D beneficiaries will get GLP-1 access via the Medicare GLP-1 Bridge from July 1, 2026 through December 31, 2027. Medicare pricing for negotiated GLP-1 injections is set at roughly $245/month, with patient copays capped at $50. TrumpRx-direct is ~$350/month. For MariTide, the implication is: if Amgen joins the model, the access lift is massive; if it doesn't, patients stay on commercial/self-pay rails. Goldman Sachs trimmed its 2030 global anti-obesity market to ~$95B from a prior $130B estimate, in part because of coverage stringency and rising discontinuation rates.

Prescription renewal length — rule of thumb

  • Chronic cardiometabolic (Repatha, MariTide future): Initial 30–90 day supply, then 90-day refills. PA typically renewed every 6–12 months.
  • Oral psoriasis / PsA (Otezla): 30-day initial, 90-day maintenance.
  • Severe-asthma / CRSwNP biologic (TEZSPIRE): PA usually re-approved every 6–12 months on exacerbation-reduction evidence.
  • Osteoporosis (Prolia, EVENITY): Prolia every 6 months — the renewal is the dose. EVENITY is a finite 12-month course.
  • Oncology (IMDELLTRA, BLINCYTO, Kyprolis): Until progression or unacceptable toxicity.
  • Rare disease infusions (TEPEZZA, UPLIZNA, KRYSTEXXA): Per protocol; UPLIZNA is twice-yearly maintenance.

Step 10 · What could go right, what could go wrong

Headwinds & tailwindsas of Feb 2026

A balanced view. The headwinds are concrete and near-term; the tailwinds are bigger in dollar potential but more conditional on execution.

Scary headwinds

  • IRA price setting — Otezla Otezla's Maximum Fair Price takes effect January 1, 2027. Amgen has already booked a $1.2B intangible-asset impairment. Future IPAY cycles will pull in more of Amgen's small-molecule franchise.
  • Prolia / XGEVA biosimilar cliff US composition patent expired February 2025; biosimilars launched from late May 2025. Prolia's $4B+ revenue base is entering structural erosion even with the late-May staggered entry.
  • $10.7B IRS dispute Shareholder class action over disclosure is proceeding; underlying IRS exams for 2016–2018 are live, with likely extensions to later years.
  • MariTide's Phase 2 GI tolerability signal Up to ~25% of participants reported vomiting at higher doses in Phase 1 PK-LDI; investigators have redesigned Phase 3 dose escalation, but Wegovy/Zepbound already hold the mindshare.
  • Enbrel's declining volume Even with patents to 2029, Enbrel is losing share to IL-17 and IL-23 class competitors. Revenue contraction is already showing up quarter to quarter.
  • LUMAKRAS under-delivery Trailing the KRAS G12C class vs expectations; Q3 2025 sales were down 2% YoY.
  • Payer stringency on obesity Goldman Sachs cut global anti-obesity TAM from $130B to $95B; cost and loss of coverage drive 60–64% of GLP-1 discontinuations today.

Exciting tailwinds

  • MariTide's differentiated profile Once-monthly dosing, ~20% mean weight loss at 52 weeks with no plateau — if Phase 3 holds, it is a credible counter-entry into the obesity blockbuster category.
  • Repatha's primary-prevention story VESALIUS-CV data (25% MACE reduction, 36% MI reduction in high-risk CV patients without prior MI/stroke) expands the eligible patient pool substantially.
  • IMDELLTRA scale-up Q3 2025 sales ~$178M (+33% QoQ), Phase 3 confirmatory overall-survival data, and trials moving into earlier lines of SCLC all point to blockbuster potential.
  • UPLIZNA label stacking Added IgG4-RD and gMG in 2025; EC approval for gMG in Feb 2026. +46% YoY sales growth in Q3 2025. Each indication compounds prescriber familiarity.
  • TEZSPIRE beyond asthma CRSwNP Phase 3 (WAYPOINT) showed 98% reduction in need for polyp surgery and 88% reduction in systemic steroid use; FDA approved late 2025. COPD and EoE Ph3 trials ongoing.
  • Biosimilars scaling to ~$4B PAVBLU, WEZLANA, BKEMV launching in sequence; Amgen targets $4B franchise by ~2030. ABP 692 (ocrelizumab, MS) in Phase 3.
  • Rare disease momentum TEPEZZA, KRYSTEXXA, UPLIZNA, TAVNEOS together generated $1.2B+ per quarter at recent runs — the Horizon acquisition is earning back.
  • GLP-1 coverage environment opening BALANCE and GLP-1 Bridge create a Medicare path for obesity drugs; employer GLP-1 obesity coverage projected to reach 65% by 2027.

How to actually use this page with a sales manager

Pair every headwind with a counter on the tailwind side when you're asked about risk. The honest executive-summary line: Amgen's base is eroding faster than some peers' (Prolia LOE + Otezla IRA), but its growth book — rare disease + TEZSPIRE + IMDELLTRA + eventually MariTide — is real and accelerating. The company's reinvention rides on MariTide converting from a hope into a product.

Step 11 · Language

Glossary — searchable, cross-linked

Every clickable term in this brief jumps here. Use the search box to filter by word or category. Filter by pill to narrow to just sales/commercial terms or just biology terms.

Sales

LOE — Loss of Exclusivity

The date a drug's market protection ends — usually via patent expiry or the end of regulatory exclusivity — after which generics (small molecules) or biosimilars (biologics) can enter.

Sales

TAM — Total Addressable Market

The full revenue opportunity if a product reached every eligible patient. In pharma usually stated as $ or patient-count; sellers then adjust for serviceable and obtainable share.

Payer

ASP — Average Sales Price

The CMS-published benchmark used to reimburse physician-administered drugs under Medicare Part B. ASP + 6% is the classic buy-and-bill formula.

Payer

Buy-and-bill

A channel where the provider (hospital, infusion center, clinic) purchases the drug, administers it, and then bills the payer. Common for IV infusions and many oncology and rare-disease drugs.

Payer

Formulary

A payer or PBM's list of covered drugs, typically arranged in tiers with different cost-sharing. Preferred brand, non-preferred, specialty, and excluded tiers drive most of the economics of a script.

Payer

Prior authorization (PA)

A utilization-management step where the prescriber must get payer approval before a drug is dispensed. PA burden is frequently the rate-limiting step on uptake.

Payer

Step therapy

A rule requiring patients to try (and fail) cheaper therapies first before coverage of a more expensive option is granted.

Regulatory

IRA — Inflation Reduction Act

A 2022 US law that, among other things, gave CMS the authority to negotiate prices for selected high-spend drugs under Medicare Part B and D. Selected drugs receive a Maximum Fair Price that takes effect in subsequent years.

Regulatory

MFP — Maximum Fair Price

The price CMS sets under IRA negotiation. For drugs selected in the first cycle, MFPs take effect January 1, 2026; Otezla's MFP under a later cycle takes effect January 1, 2027.

Regulatory

BPCIA — Biologics Price Competition and Innovation Act

The 2009 US law that created the biosimilar regulatory pathway, including a "patent dance" procedure biosimilar developers and originators use to litigate patent issues before launch.

Biology

Biosimilar

A biological product that is highly similar to, and has no clinically meaningful differences from, an already-approved reference biologic. Not an exact copy — biologics are too complex for that — but therapeutically equivalent.

Biology

Biosimilar (alt spelling)

See Biosimilar above.

Regulatory

Interchangeable designation

An FDA status granted to certain biosimilars, allowing a pharmacist to substitute the biosimilar for the reference biologic without prescriber intervention, subject to state law.

Biology

Reference product

The originator biologic a biosimilar is compared against (e.g., Humira is the reference product for AMJEVITA).

Biology

GLP-1 receptor agonist

A molecule that mimics glucagon-like peptide-1 and activates its receptor, producing insulin release, glucagon suppression, slowed gastric emptying, and reduced appetite via central mechanisms.

Biology

GIP receptor (GIPR)

The receptor for glucose-dependent insulinotropic polypeptide. Can be either agonized (Zepbound) or antagonized (MariTide) to contribute to weight loss — the mechanism debate is live.

Biology

Peptide-antibody conjugate

A single molecule combining a peptide (often a GLP-1 analog) with a full monoclonal antibody via covalent linkers. The antibody extends half-life; the peptide delivers the receptor activity. MariTide is an example.

Biology

PCSK9

Proprotein convertase subtilisin/kexin type 9 — a protein that degrades LDL receptors. Blocking it keeps LDL receptors on hepatocytes longer, lowering circulating LDL-C. Target of Repatha.

Biology

LDL-C

Low-density lipoprotein cholesterol — the "bad" cholesterol most strongly linked to atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease. Reducing LDL-C reduces event risk.

Biology

MACE

Major adverse cardiovascular events — a composite clinical endpoint typically comprising CV death, non-fatal MI, and non-fatal stroke; sometimes expanded to include hospitalization for unstable angina or heart failure.

Biology

ASCVD

Atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease — the umbrella for coronary artery disease, cerebrovascular disease, and peripheral artery disease driven by plaque buildup.

Biology

RANKL

Receptor activator of nuclear factor kappa-B ligand — a cytokine that tells osteoclast precursors to mature and resorb bone. Blocking RANKL (Prolia/XGEVA) slows bone loss.

Biology

Sclerostin

A protein secreted by osteocytes that inhibits Wnt-signaling–driven bone formation. Blocking it (EVENITY) allows bones to build.

Biology

BiTE — Bispecific T-cell Engager

A recombinant bispecific molecule designed to bridge a tumor antigen (e.g., DLL3, CD19) on one arm with CD3 on a T cell on the other, physically redirecting T-cell cytotoxicity to the tumor. Amgen is the originator of the platform.

Biology

DLL3

Delta-like ligand 3 — a Notch-pathway ligand highly expressed in small-cell lung cancer and largely absent from normal tissue. Target of IMDELLTRA.

Biology

KRAS G12C

A specific glycine-to-cysteine mutation at codon 12 of the KRAS oncogene. The cysteine residue creates a covalent drug-binding pocket, enabling the first generation of KRAS inhibitors (sotorasib/LUMAKRAS, adagrasib).

Biology

ES-SCLC

Extensive-stage small-cell lung cancer — SCLC that has spread beyond one lung at diagnosis. Aggressive, chemo-responsive initially, but historically poor survival in later lines.

Biology

Metastatic CRC

Metastatic colorectal cancer. LUMAKRAS + Vectibix is approved for 3L KRAS G12C–mutated mCRC.

Biology

B-ALL

B-cell acute lymphoblastic leukemia — an aggressive blood cancer of B-cell precursors. BLINCYTO is approved across relapsed, MRD-positive, and frontline consolidation settings.

Biology

CRS — Cytokine Release Syndrome

A systemic inflammatory response to T-cell-engaging therapies (BiTEs, CAR-T). Monitoring and stepwise dosing are central to safe administration.

Biology

TSLP

Thymic stromal lymphopoietin — an epithelial alarmin that sits upstream of multiple Th2 and non-Th2 inflammatory cascades. Blocking it (TEZSPIRE) is effective across asthma phenotypes.

Biology

IL-12/23

Interleukins that drive Th1 and Th17 immune responses. Stelara (ustekinumab, targeted by Amgen's WEZLANA) binds the shared p40 subunit and is used in psoriasis, PsA, Crohn's, and UC.

Biology

PDE4 inhibitor

Phosphodiesterase-4 inhibitor. Raises intracellular cAMP in immune cells, dampening cytokine production. Otezla is a selective PDE4 inhibitor.

Biology

Fusion protein

A recombinant protein created by joining two genes encoding different protein domains. Enbrel fuses the extracellular portion of the TNF receptor with an IgG1 Fc region, creating a soluble TNF decoy.

Biology

Proteasome inhibitor

Blocks the proteasome, the cellular machine that degrades old and misfolded proteins. Myeloma cells are particularly sensitive. Kyprolis is an epoxyketone-class inhibitor; bortezomib was first-in-class.

Biology

Thrombopoietin receptor (MPL)

The receptor that drives platelet production. Nplate (romiplostim) mimics thrombopoietin to raise platelet counts in chronic ITP.

Biology

IGF-1 receptor

Insulin-like growth factor-1 receptor. Overexpressed on orbital fibroblasts in thyroid eye disease. TEPEZZA blocks it to shrink proptosis and reduce diplopia.

Biology

TED — Thyroid Eye Disease

An autoimmune orbital inflammatory disease, often linked to Graves' disease, causing proptosis (bulging eyes), redness, double vision, and in severe cases vision loss.

Biology

CD19

A B-cell surface marker expressed across a broad B-cell lineage, including plasmablasts. UPLIZNA depletes CD19+ cells in NMOSD, IgG4-RD, and gMG.

Biology

C5aR1

The complement component 5a receptor 1, a GPCR expressed on neutrophils driving vasculitis inflammation. TAVNEOS (avacopan) is a selective oral antagonist.

Biology

ANCA-associated vasculitis

A group of autoimmune small-vessel vasculitides including granulomatosis with polyangiitis (GPA) and microscopic polyangiitis (MPA), driven by anti-neutrophil cytoplasmic antibodies.

Biology

PEGylation

Covalent attachment of polyethylene glycol chains to a protein, extending circulating half-life and reducing immunogenicity. KRYSTEXXA is PEGylated uricase.

Biology

VEGF-A

Vascular endothelial growth factor A — drives pathological angiogenesis in tumors and wet AMD. Targets for Avastin (bevacizumab, copied by MVASI) and Eylea (aflibercept, copied by PAVBLU).

Biology

CHO cell

Chinese hamster ovary cell line — the workhorse mammalian expression system for recombinant therapeutic antibodies, including most Amgen mAbs.

Administration

Subcutaneous (SC)

Injection into the fat layer below the skin. Slower absorption than IV; enables self-administration via pens and autoinjectors.

Biology

Adverse event (AE)

Any unfavorable medical occurrence in a patient administered a therapy, whether or not causally related. Serious AEs (SAEs) are those causing death, hospitalization, or lasting disability.

Biology

NNT — Number Needed to Treat

The number of patients who must be treated to prevent one additional event. Lower is better. A common cardiology shorthand when evaluating incremental value of a new agent.

Drug

Repatha — evolocumab

PCSK9-inhibiting monoclonal antibody, SC q2w/q4w. VESALIUS-CV showed a 25% MACE reduction in primary-prevention-eligible patients.

Drug

MariTide — maridebart cafraglutide

Phase 3 investigational GLP-1 RA / GIPR antagonist peptide-antibody conjugate; monthly SC injection for obesity, T2D, OSA, heart failure, and ASCVD.

Drug

Prolia / XGEVA — denosumab

Anti-RANKL mAb in two dosing regimens: osteoporosis (Prolia, 60 mg SC q6m) and cancer bone mets / GCTB (XGEVA).

Drug

EVENITY — romosozumab-aqqg

Anti-sclerostin mAb for postmenopausal osteoporosis at high fracture risk. Finite 12-month course, two SC injections monthly.

Drug

IMDELLTRA — tarlatamab-dlle

DLL3 × CD3 BiTE for 2L+ ES-SCLC. IV with step-up dosing and CRS monitoring.

Drug

LUMAKRAS — sotorasib

First-in-class oral KRAS G12C covalent inhibitor for NSCLC and, with Vectibix, 3L KRAS G12C mCRC.

Drug

BLINCYTO — blinatumomab

CD19 × CD3 BiTE for B-ALL; continuous IV infusion. Expanding in frontline MRD-positive settings.

Drug

TEZSPIRE — tezepelumab-ekko

Anti-TSLP biologic, SC q4w. Approved in severe asthma and CRSwNP; trials ongoing in COPD and EoE.

Drug

Otezla — apremilast

Oral PDE4 inhibitor for psoriasis, PsA, Behçet's oral ulcers. Selected for Medicare price negotiation with MFP from Jan 1, 2027.

Drug

Enbrel — etanercept

TNF receptor–Fc fusion protein, SC weekly. Biosimilars held off the US market until 2029 through litigation.

Drug

TEPEZZA — teprotumumab-trbw

Anti-IGF-1R mAb for TED. IV q3w × 8. Hearing-AE monitoring required.

Drug

KRYSTEXXA — pegloticase

PEGylated uricase enzyme for refractory gout. IV q2w; often given with methotrexate to mitigate immunogenicity.

Drug

UPLIZNA — inebilizumab-cdon

Anti-CD19 B-cell depleter, IV. Approved in NMOSD, IgG4-RD, gMG. Maintenance every 6 months.

Drug

TAVNEOS — avacopan

Oral C5aR1 antagonist for ANCA-associated vasculitis, reducing steroid exposure in chronic therapy.
